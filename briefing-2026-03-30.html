🚨 The Kharg Island Threat

"We will conclude our lovely 'stay' in Iran by blowing up and completely obliterating all of their Electric Generating Plants, Oil Wells and Kharg Island (and possibly all desalinization plants!)" — Trump, Truth Social, March 30
"My favourite thing is to take the oil in Iran... Maybe we take Kharg Island, maybe we don't." — Trump, Financial Times interview

New target: desalination plants. This crosses into civilian infrastructure / collective punishment territory. Either maximum-pressure negotiating or actual target planning.

Multi-Step Reasoning: Kharg Island

  1. Kharg handles ~90% of Iran's oil exports ($50B+/yr). Destroying it = most devastating single strike possible
  2. BUT destroying Kharg removes ~1.5-2M bbl/day from global supply permanently — self-defeating if goal is lower oil
  3. FT "take the oil" quote suggests seizure not destruction. Control Iran's revenue. Iraq 2.0 playbook
  4. Seizure requires Marines (confirmed in Sunday Pentagon reports)
  5. Iran's response would be total — everything remaining at Gulf infrastructure
  6. Bond market pricing this as deflationary (10Y yield DOWN) — global recession > inflation
  7. Stock market UP (+0.7%) = investors think this is bluster. If they believed Kharg was real, we'd see -3-5%

Iraq Force Majeure — The Hidden Bombshell

On March 20, Iraq declared force majeure on all foreign-operated oilfields — 4.5M bbl/day (OPEC's 2nd largest producer) out of reliable supply. Combined with Hormuz closure (~17.8M bbl/day flow disrupted), total disruption approaches ~22M bbl/day or 22% of global production. [FXLeaders]

30Y Treasury — Ken's Position Update

The 9bps drop in 10Y yield (4.44% → 4.35%) is the first meaningful bond rally since the war began. The paradox: the WORSE the war gets (ground ops, Kharg, recession), the BETTER the 30Y trade performs via flight-to-safety. A diplomatic resolution that crashes oil would also be bullish (disinflation → cuts). The losing scenario is the middle — war drags, oil $100-120, no recession, no cuts. That remains the base case.
