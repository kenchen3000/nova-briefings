Geopolitical & Political News

The market is now trading possible talks alongside an ongoing blockade. Reuters/LSEG still shows a large oil premium, while Trading Economics says the U.S. and Iran maintained their blockade on Hormuz even as Pakistan signaled an Iranian delegation could be open to talks this weekend.

Why it matters: if the route stays constrained, oil remains a tax on consumers and margins. If talks produce a real operating change, not just a headline, that premium can fade quickly. For now, the market is not granting diplomacy full credibility.

AI & Technology

AI demand still looks strong. TSMC reported $35.9B of Q1 revenue, up 40.6% y/y. Amazon previously said AWS AI revenue run rate exceeded $15B. Reuters reporting via WHBL says Intel stock was up 29% premarket and the company’s market value was set to move above $420B, a sign that AI inference demand is broadening beyond the pure GPU story.

Signal: this is bullish for AI fundamentals, but the market is still rewarding monetizers before speculative beta.
